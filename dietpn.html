Diet for Pneumonia

1. Whole Grains
  • The carbohydrate content in whole grains like quinoa, brown rice, oats, barley provides energy to the body during this time.
2. Protein-Rich Foods
  • A diet rich in protein is beneficial for people suffering from pneumonia. Foods like nuts, seeds, beans, white meat and cold-water fishes like salmon and sardines have anti-inflammatory properties.
3. Green Leafy Vegetables
  • Green leafy vegetables such as kale, lettuce, spinach are highly packed with nutrients that help in the healing of this respiratory infection. They contain antioxidants that protect the body against infectious agents.
4. Citrus Fruits
  • Vitamin C-rich fruits like oranges, berries, kiwi can help boost your immune system and thus improve recovery. The antioxidants in them can protect your body against pathogens that cause infections.
5. Honey
  • Honey is famous for its healing properties. It has anti-bacterial properties that help in aiding cough and cold which are the symptoms of pneumonia.
6. Turmeric
  • Turmeric has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce chest pain which is one of the preliminary symptoms of pneumonia. Turmeric acts as a mucolytic, which means it helps in removing the mucus and catarrh from the bronchial ducts which leads to easy breathing.
7. Ginger
  • Ginger is also known for its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. It helps in reducing chest pain which is a symptom of pneumonia infection.
8. Fenugreek Tea
  • This tea might help in curing the persistent cough one experiences in pneumonia. Also, researches show that fenugreek seeds can help a person in sweating which reduces their body temperature during fever.
9. Probiotics
  • Foods like yoghurt contain the probiotics which inhibit the growth of the pneumonia-causing pathogen.
  • They also boost the immune system as they increase the good bacteria in the gut.
10. Water
  • Drinking plenty of fluids is highly recommended if you are suffering from pneumonia. Not only water but other fluids like juices help in loosening the mucus from the lungs.
  • They flush out the harmful toxins and help in getting rid of the foreign particles that cause an obstruction in the respiratory tract.

What to avoid?








1. Excessive Salt
  • While a small pinch of salt cooked in a dish may be fine, a salt-heavy diet can be a problem. Salt can make people retain water, and excess water can cause breathing problems.
  • Instead of using salt or a salt substitute, try herbs and spices to enhance the flavour of food
2. Beer
  • In general, alcohol can increase inflammation in the lungs.
  • For those with poor lung health, beer is a bad choice specifically because it’s also carbonated and can cause bloating, which puts extra pressure on the lungs and can make it harder to breathe.
3. Processed Meats
  • Cured meats and cold cuts contain nitrates, which companies often add to these products for color and to help extend shelf life.
  • These nitrates, however, have been found to lead to an increase in Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD).
4. Soda
  • Drinking soda can be harmful to those with lung disease in several ways. First, soda contains carbon dioxide to make it carbonated, which can cause gas and bloating that makes it harder to breathe.
  • In addition, the high sugar content can increase inflammation and lead to weight gain, both of which can exacerbate COPD symptoms
  • To reduce soda intake: Try switching soda out with other noncarbonated, flavored beverages like tea, flavored water or natural juice.
5. Fried Foods
  • Fried foods such as french fries and onion rings contain unhealthy fats that can cause bloating and discomfort by pushing on the diaphragm.
  • In addition to lung discomfort caused by bloating, fried foods over time can lead to higher cholesterol and weight gain. For people living with lung disease, these things can worsen symptoms.
  • To reduce fried food intake: Avoid fast-food chains and drive-thru meals.
6. White Bread
  • Simple carbohydrates such as white bread should be avoided, as it takes more work for the lungs to metabolize them.
  • Switching out these simple carbs for whole-grain, complex carbohydrates can improve lung health.
7. Chocolate
  • First and foremost, chocolate contains caffeine, which can interfere with medication or increase heart rate.
  • Chocolate is also high in sugar and low in nutrients, making it a generally bad choice for someone with lung disease.
8. Dairy Products
  • As the body digests dairy products, a breakdown of milk digestion called casomorphin increases the amount of phlegm and mucus produced by the body. This can increase coughing, which can cause wheezing and pain in COPD patients.
  • To reduce dairy intake: Trade lactose for milk alternatives such as almond, oat or soy milk.
